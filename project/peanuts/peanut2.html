“Each year, the fashion industry produces 100 billion garments, with 92 million tonnes discarded in landfills. This equates to a garbage truck’s worth of clothing being dumped every second (Ellen MacArthur Foundation, n.d.).”

As a practice-based designer, I position myself not as a problem-solver, but as a mediator - connecting disciplines and using material experimentation as a way to speculate on more sustainable futures.

How can designers challenge the unsustainable fashion paradigm through the creation of a garment combining digital fabrication tools and craftsmanship technique?

This handbag merges 3D printing and crochet as a small, tangible experiment proving that alternative methods of production are already possible.
The process revealed fragmented systems and clarified the role of the designer as one who connects, questions, and navigates complexity through making.
